About Me

I am an Electrical Engineering student at Georgia Tech interested in building systems where hardware and software have to work together under real performance constraints. I am especially drawn to the parts of computing that happen close to the metal: architecture, low-latency systems, embedded platforms, digital design, and performance-critical infrastructure.

My interests sit around high-performance computing, VLSI, FPGAs, ASICs, computer architecture, and low-level systems. I like understanding how computation actually moves through a system — from circuits and chips, to operating systems and scheduling, to the software layers that make real-time decisions.

Outside of class, I like building projects that force me to connect theory with implementation. Some of my work includes real-time scheduler simulation, robotics, anomaly detection pipelines, media-processing tools, and hardware-oriented projects involving embedded firmware and control.

C++ • CNNs • FPGA/ASIC • Vitis HLS • Dashboard

FPGA Convolution Neural Network Accelerator in C++ Vitis HLS

Built a C++/Vitis HLS CNN inference accelerator targeting FPGA synthesis, implementing Q8.8 fixed-point 3×3 convolution, ReLU, and max pooling with tiled tensor buffering, loop pipelining, MAC-array unrolling, array partitioning, and top-level DATAFLOW. Synthesized on a Zynq UltraScale+ target at 100 MHz, the optimized design reduced end-to-end inference latency from 1062 to 716 cycles, achieving a 1.48× improvement while using 9 DSPs, 6 BRAMs, 6310 FFs, and 5722 LUTs.
